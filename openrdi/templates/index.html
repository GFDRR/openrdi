The Open Data for Resilience Initiative (OpenDRI) seeks to promote data sharing and provide support to decision making in order to build resilience to disasters in a changing climate. It is a dynamic partnership between private companies, academic institutions, and governments that achieves this by the development of tools and a participatory approach to technical assistance.

The initiative is the result of the first policy recommendation of the joint World Bank - UN report, "Natural Hazards, UnNatural Disasters, the Economics of Effective Prevention", which stressed the importance of data sharing to reduce vulnerabilities to disaster.

OpenDRI also builds upon the World Bank's Open Data Initiative. The motivation of the Bank's broader open data initiative is to promote transparency, accountability, and availability of data to support improved decision making. OpenDRI is currently implementing these ideas in 25 countries around the world to improve disaster and climate change resilience. Our full public launch will be in April 2012. Sharing data to rebuild Haiti

An example of OpenDRI in action is haitidata.org. This website is built using a free and open source software tool, GeoNode, and makes risk assessment data produced following the 2010 Haiti earthquake available for anyone to download and use. This tool enables individuals and organizations to contribute data, thereby enabling people to collaborate and and utilize these data required to rebuild Haiti.
